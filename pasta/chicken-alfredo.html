Chicken Alfredo

Ingredients:

For the pasta

  • 16 ounces dry fettuccine pasta

The Chicken

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 teaspoon italian seasoning
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter

For the Sauce

  • 1/2 cup butter, cut into large cubes or slices
  • 2 cups heavy whippin cream
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 3/4 teaspoon garlic power
  • 3/4 teaspoon italian seasoning
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • teaspoon pepper
  • 2 cups freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Make the noodles: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add the fettuccine and cook until al dente according to the packeage directions, usually 10 minutes. reserve 1/2 cup of cooking water, then drain well. Set aside.
  2. Make the chicken: Season chicken breasts with the italian seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook the chicken:Warm the olive oil over medium-high heat in a larg nonstick skillet. Once it's shimmering, swirl the pan to evenly distribute. Add the chicken and leave it undisturbed for 5-7 minutes, until the bottom is golden-brown. Flip ovewr and add in 1 tablespoon of butter between them, picking up the pan to give it a gentle swirl to distribute. Continue cooking for another 5-7 minutes (or an internal temperature reaches 165 defrees F.)

Tips:

  • Avoid pre-shedded cheese. For best results and taste, use real Parmigiano-Reggiano right off the block.
  • Don't over cook the chicken. Use an instant-read thermometer so you can know exactly when the chicken is done and you don't have to guess.
  • The pasta. A long, flat noodle like Fettucine is perfect here. Linguini and penne are also good options for pasta.
  • have everything ready before you start, Time can go by fast and you might find yourself rushing to find ingredients if you don't have them ready when you start.
  • Sauce too thing? You can either simmer for a few extra minutes or take it off the heat and let it sit for a minute or two before you mix it with the pasta. Alternetively, if you think it is too thick, add some reserved pasta water to loosen it up a bit.
  • Want to lighten it up>This is a high calorie meal and if you want to die it down a little, use half-n-half or whole milk instead of heavy whipping cream. Just remember, the lower the fat content, the thinner the sauce.
  • Cut the chicken: Transfer the chicken to a cutting board and let rest for 3 minutes. Cut into 1/2-inch-thick slices. Tent with foil while you prepare the sauce.
  • Make the Alfredo sauce: In the same pan, over medium-low heat, add the butter and cream; whisk until butter has melted.
